About

Mens Den is a men's peer support group set up in May 2018.

We meet at

Colne Open Door
, Wednesday evening between 6pm and 7:30pm.

Mens Den is 100% confidential, totally non-judgemental and open to men of all ages, regardless of race, disability etc.

We work with closely with a number of local organisations which include:

  • Samaritans
  • Up and Active Pendle
  • Open Door
  • Elisha House
  • Citadel
  • Curry on the Street
  • MS Angling

The group continues to evolve as we understand it may not always be for everyone, some people are regulars and others drop in as and when they need some support; but what always remains is a passion to help our fellow man by giving them the time to talk about what is going on in their life and giving them an opportunity to get whatever is bothering them off their chest.

We run a private WhatsApp group available to those who attend the group. This gives each chap additional support away from the weekly meeting should they find themselves in a spot of difficulty.

In the past we have had talks from local groups and individuals which have included the following:

  • Chris Kirkland, ex Liverpool keeper
  • Steven Cunningham, Curzon Ashton manager
  • Maj Pajie, Walk and Talk
  • Alison Jones, social prescriber
  • Colne Football club
  • Scott Cunliffe, who ran from Turn Moor to every Premier League away ground in 2018/2019

The group has also been nominated in the past for a number of NHS and diversity awards. Funding has been given by other local XXX.
